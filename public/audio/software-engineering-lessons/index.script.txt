Let's sit down and talk about software engineering lessons I've learned along the way. One of the main goals for this year was to put all these lessons into a format that's easy to reference. So, keep coming back for updates.

First off, let's talk about code reviews. They're really important, so do them. Learn from others and share your own experiences too. Code reviews help catch mistakes early on and improve the overall quality of your code.

Now, have you ever heard of flight rules? It's a concept where you document common problems and their solutions in a manual. This is especially useful for teams working together. For example, GitHub has a great repository called "git-flight-rules" that shows how to do this with Git.

Another thing I've learned is the importance of automating routine tasks. Use scripts or tools like bash, autohotkey, or powershell to save time and reduce context switching. This can be especially helpful for repetitive tasks that take up a lot of your time.

Keeping current with industry trends and technologies is also crucial. Listen to podcasts, dev blogs, and news feeds to stay on top of things. I recommend starting with some popular ones like "The Core Skills" podcast.

When it comes to tools, don't underestimate the value of having the right equipment for your job. Invest in a comfortable chair, keyboard, mouse, software, cell phone, and desk. Having the right tools can really boost productivity.

Another lesson I've learned is that success often follows people who create a work routine for themselves. Anyone can do this with a bit of willpower and patience. My own work routine includes deep work, reviewing, communication follow-up, meetings, lunch, and more. You might find it helpful to try out a similar schedule.

Finally, don't be afraid to learn from others' successes and mistakes. Read about how people made errors and what they did wrong. This can help you avoid making the same mistakes in your own projects.

That's all for now. Keep checking back for more software engineering lessons and updates.
