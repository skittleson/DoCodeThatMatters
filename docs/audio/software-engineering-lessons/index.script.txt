As a software engineer, you've probably learned a thing or two about what works and what doesn't in the field. My goal for this year was to take all those lessons and put them into a format that's easy to reference.

One of the most important things I've learned is the importance of code reviews. It's crucial that you do it regularly, whether it's with your team or by yourself. Code reviews help catch mistakes, improve code quality, and even speed up development in the long run.

When it comes to productivity, having the right tools can make a huge difference. A comfortable chair, keyboard, mouse, software, cell phone – all these things can increase your efficiency. But don't just stop at the physical stuff; invest in yourself too. Take courses, attend workshops, and read books on topics you're interested in.

Another key thing to remember is that failure is a part of the learning process. It's not about being perfect, it's about getting better with each attempt. So don't be discouraged when things go wrong – ask why, figure out what went wrong, and use that as an opportunity to learn.

Now, I want to share some resources that have helped me along the way. First off, there are some amazing books on software development that you should check out. "97 Things Every Programmer Should Know" is a great place to start, followed by "Adaptive Code". If you're interested in design patterns or legacy code management, then "Working Effectively with Legacy Code" is a must-read.

I also want to mention the importance of staying up-to-date with industry trends and best practices. Listen to podcasts like "Top 7 Podcasts for the Modern Software Developer", follow dev blogs, and keep an eye on what's happening in the world of tech.

And finally, don't be afraid to take risks and try new things. Working with different people can give you insight into how to collaborate differently, and creating a work routine that works for you is key to success. Just remember to stay uncomfortable – it's only by pushing ourselves outside our comfort zones that we truly grow as developers.
